# Judge Q&A Prep

This document is for fast live answers during demos, interviews, and judging.

Keep the tone calm, sharp, and practical.

---

## 1. What is Bibipilot?

### Short answer
Bibipilot is a Binance-native copilot that checks what’s moving, filters what’s dangerous, and publishes what’s worth attention.

### Longer answer
It uses Binance Skills as the evidence layer, adds a judgment layer on top, and then turns that into chat-ready and Binance Square-ready output.

---

## 2. What problem are you solving?

### Short answer
Crypto users have too much raw attention and too little clean judgment.

### Longer answer
People can already see token movement, wallet activity, smart-money signals, and market ranking. The real problem is deciding what is real, what is risky, what is late, and what is worth sharing. Bibipilot is built for that gap.

---

## 3. What makes this different from just using ChatGPT?

### Short answer
ChatGPT can talk about crypto. Bibipilot is built around Binance-native evidence, command-specific workflows, and live publishing.

### Longer answer
The difference is product structure. Bibipilot has a real command surface, Binance-skill-backed inputs, audit-aware behavior, and a direct Binance Square publishing loop. It is not just a general model with a crypto prompt.

---

## 4. Why is this Binance-native?

### Short answer
Because the product is built around Binance Skills for evidence and Binance Square for output.

### Longer answer
The architecture story is simple: Binance Skills provide token, signal, audit, wallet, and market-rank evidence. Bibipilot adds judgment. Binance Square becomes the publishing layer. That is much more native than a generic crypto assistant.

---

## 5. Is this live, or mostly concept?

### Short answer
It is already partially live, not just concept.

### Longer answer
Live Binance Square posting works, the scheduled premium 1-post/day Square engine works, the command surface is real, and the bridge is partially live for token, signal, audit, and watchtoday flows.

---

## 6. What is already working today?

### Short answer
Real commands, real Square publishing, real scheduling, and a partially live bridge.

### Longer answer
Bibipilot already supports `/price`, `/brief`, `/risk`, `/audit`, `/token`, `/signal`, `/wallet`, `/watchtoday`, and `/meme`, and it has live posting proof on Binance Square.

---

## 7. Why not make it a trading bot?

### Short answer
Because the strongest product role right now is judgment, not autonomous execution.

### Longer answer
Execution is higher risk and easier to overclaim. Bibipilot is sharper as the layer between raw market signals and usable conviction. That is a stronger and safer product story.

---

## 8. What is the real innovation here?

### Short answer
Turning Binance Skills outputs into judgment, then turning that judgment into publishable output.

### Longer answer
The strongest innovation is not one model trick. It is the workflow: research -> judgment -> publishing. That makes Bibipilot feel like a product instead of a raw crypto summarizer.

---

## 9. How do you handle risk and safety?

### Short answer
By making risk first-class instead of decorative.

### Longer answer
Bibipilot includes audit-aware behavior, standalone `/audit`, clearer risk-first commands, and more honest handling when evidence is weak or missing. The product tries to avoid turning weak evidence into hype.

---

### Short answer

### Longer answer
A lot of crypto tools surface excitement before security. Bibipilot gives audit its own command surface so users can inspect risk directly.

Many tools stop at internal dashboards or chat answers. Bibipilot goes one step further and turns judgment into live Binance Square posts. That closes the loop.
